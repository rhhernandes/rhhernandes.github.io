About

I am an artificial intelligence (AI) ethics researcher and data journalist specializing in the intersections between AI, journalism, and society. My work combines in-depth reporting and quantitative analysis to explore the ethical implications and societal impacts of AI technologies.

I hold a master’s degree in Ethics of AI, Data, and Algorithms from the University of Cambridge, graduating with distinction and receiving the Huw Price Prize for best overall performance in my cohort. My research addresses how AI reshapes journalism, information environments, and epistemic security.

My journalism career includes roles as a data journalist at The Guardian (UK) and as an editor-at-large at Folha de S.Paulo (Brazil), where I led innovative data- and AI-driven newsroom projects and covered key topics at the intersection of technology and society.

As a Research Assistant at the Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence (LCFI) at Cambridge, I analyzed media coverage of AI and examined the use of generative AI in political communication, also contributing to a toolkit designed to help journalists responsibly navigate and implement AI technologies.

I regularly speak at technology and journalism conferences, engaging diverse audiences to foster critical understanding of AI’s role in society. I am committed to empowering the public through technology, data, and informed discourse on AI ethics and responsible innovation.
